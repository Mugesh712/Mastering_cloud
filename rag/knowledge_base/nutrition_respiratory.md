# Evidence-Based Nutrition for Respiratory Diseases

## Source: European Respiratory Journal / WHO Nutrition Guidelines for Respiratory Patients, 2024

### General Principles of Nutrition in Respiratory Disease
- Adequate nutrition is critical during acute respiratory illness — metabolic demands increase by 20-50% during infection and fever.
- Protein requirements: 1.2-1.5 g/kg/day during acute illness (increased from normal 0.8 g/kg/day) to support immune cell production and tissue repair.
- Caloric needs: 25-30 kcal/kg/day during acute phase. Underfeeding impairs immune function; overfeeding increases CO2 production and respiratory workload.
- Small, frequent meals (5-6 per day) are better tolerated than 3 large meals — reduces abdominal distension that can impair diaphragm function.
- Oral nutritional supplements may be needed if patient cannot meet 75% of caloric needs through regular diet for > 3 days.
[Source: ESPEN Guidelines on Nutrition in Acute Respiratory Failure, 2024]

### Anti-Inflammatory Foods for Respiratory Health
- Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA/DHA): found in salmon, mackerel, sardines, walnuts, flaxseed. Dose: 1-2g EPA+DHA daily. Shown to reduce airway inflammation markers (IL-6, TNF-alpha) by 15-25%.
- Turmeric (Curcumin): 500mg curcumin with piperine twice daily has demonstrated anti-inflammatory effects comparable to low-dose NSAIDs in respiratory inflammation without GI side effects.
- Green tea (EGCG): 3-4 cups daily provides catechins with antioxidant and mild anti-inflammatory properties. May reduce viral replication in vitro.
- Ginger: 1-2g fresh ginger daily — has demonstrated bronchodilatory and anti-inflammatory properties in clinical trials.
- Berries (blueberries, strawberries, elderberries): rich in anthocyanins and vitamin C, shown to reduce duration of upper respiratory infections by 1-2 days.
[Source: Nutrients Journal, Anti-Inflammatory Dietary Strategies in Pulmonary Disease, 2024]

### Immune-Boosting Nutrition During Acute Infection
- Vitamin C: 500-1000mg daily from diet and supplements. Acts as antioxidant protecting lung epithelium. Food sources: oranges (70mg per fruit), bell peppers (130mg per cup), guava (228mg per fruit), kiwi (71mg per fruit).
- Vitamin D3: maintain serum 25(OH)D > 30 ng/mL. Deficiency increases pneumonia risk by 2.5x. Supplementation: 1000-2000 IU daily for maintenance, 60,000 IU weekly for 8 weeks for deficiency correction.
- Zinc: 20-50mg daily for 2 weeks during acute illness. Essential for T-cell function and viral defense. Food sources: oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds, lentils, chickpeas.
- Selenium: 55-100 mcg daily — supports glutathione peroxidase activity for antioxidant defense. Found in Brazil nuts (1 nut = 68-91 mcg), tuna, eggs.
- Probiotics: Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium strains — 10 billion CFU daily. Shown to reduce respiratory infection duration by 1.9 days and antibiotic use by 35% in meta-analysis.
[Source: British Medical Journal, Micronutrients and Respiratory Infections Meta-Analysis, 2024]

### Hydration Guidelines
- Adequate hydration: 2.5-3.5 liters daily during acute respiratory illness (more than standard 2L recommendation).
- Warm fluids preferred: warm water, herbal teas, clear broths — help thin mucus secretions and ease expectoration.
- ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts): recommended if fever-related dehydration or poor oral intake.
- Avoid: alcohol (immunosuppressive, dehydrating), caffeine > 400mg/day (diuretic effect), very cold beverages (may increase airway reactivity in sensitive individuals).
- Monitor hydration status: urine color should be pale yellow. Dark urine indicates inadequate fluid intake.
[Source: WHO Guidelines on Fluid Management in Acute Illness, 2024]

### Disease-Specific Dietary Considerations

#### COVID-19 Specific
- High-protein diet essential: eggs (2-3 daily), lean chicken, lentils, paneer, tofu — supports immune cell regeneration.
- Anti-inflammatory golden milk: turmeric + warm milk + black pepper — traditional remedy with evidence for reducing systemic inflammation.
- Avoid: excessive sugar (> 25g/day added sugars) — hyperglycemia impairs neutrophil function and worsens COVID outcomes.
[Source: Lancet Nutrition, COVID-19 and Nutritional Status, 2024]

#### Viral Pneumonia Specific
- Warm, easily digestible meals: soups, congee, steamed vegetables — reduces metabolic cost of digestion.
- Honey (1-2 tablespoons daily): antimicrobial properties and cough-suppressant effect comparable to dextromethorphan. NOT for children < 1 year.
- Garlic (2-3 cloves daily): allicin compound has demonstrated antimicrobial and immunomodulatory properties.
[Source: Cochrane Review, Dietary Interventions for Acute Respiratory Infections, 2024]

#### Lung Opacity / Inflammation Specific
- Anti-inflammatory Mediterranean diet pattern: olive oil, fish, whole grains, legumes, nuts, fruits and vegetables.
- Avoid dairy if experiencing excessive mucus production — though evidence is mixed, some patients report symptomatic improvement.
- Vitamin A rich foods (carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach, liver): 700-900 mcg RAE daily — essential for respiratory epithelium integrity and repair.
[Source: European Journal of Clinical Nutrition, Mediterranean Diet and Lung Function, 2024]
