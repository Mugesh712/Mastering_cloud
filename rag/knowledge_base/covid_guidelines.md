# COVID-19 Clinical Management Guidelines

## Source: WHO Clinical Management of COVID-19 — Living Guideline, 2025 Revision 4

### Radiological Findings in COVID-19
- Bilateral ground-glass opacities (GGO) are the hallmark imaging finding in COVID-19 pneumonia, found in 85-90% of confirmed cases.
- Distribution is predominantly peripheral and posterior, involving lower lobes more frequently.
- Consolidation may develop in later stages (typically Day 7-14 of illness), indicating disease progression.
- Crazy-paving pattern (GGO with superimposed interlobular septal thickening) appears in 5-36% of cases.
- Pleural effusion is uncommon in COVID-19 (seen in <5% of cases) and when present, may suggest secondary bacterial infection or heart failure.
- Cardiac silhouette is typically normal unless pre-existing cardiomegaly is present.
[Source: WHO COVID-19 Imaging Guidelines, 2025 Rev. 4, Section 3.2]

### Antiviral Therapy Protocol
- Remdesivir (Veklury): 200mg IV on Day 1, then 100mg IV daily for 5 days (extend to 10 days for severe cases). Indicated for hospitalized patients requiring supplemental oxygen.
- Nirmatrelvir/Ritonavir (Paxlovid): 300mg/100mg orally twice daily for 5 days. Must be initiated within 5 days of symptom onset. Contraindicated with CYP3A4-dependent medications.
- Molnupiravir: 800mg orally twice daily for 5 days. Alternative when Paxlovid is contraindicated.
- Dexamethasone: 6mg IV or oral daily for up to 10 days for patients on oxygen or mechanical ventilation. NOT recommended for mild cases without hypoxia.
[Source: WHO Therapeutics and COVID-19 Living Guideline, 2025, Strong Recommendation]

### Oxygen Therapy and Monitoring
- Target SpO2: 92-96% for most patients. For pregnant patients, target SpO2 >= 95%.
- Awake prone positioning for 2-4 hours daily has shown 25% reduction in intubation rates in moderate-severe cases.
- High-flow nasal cannula (HFNC) preferred over conventional oxygen for patients with SpO2 < 90% despite standard oxygen therapy.
- Escalation to non-invasive ventilation (NIV) if HFNC fails within 1-2 hours.
- Intubation criteria: SpO2 < 88% despite HFNC/NIV, respiratory rate > 35/min, or clinical signs of respiratory exhaustion.
[Source: WHO COVID-19 Clinical Management, Chapter 10: Oxygen Therapy]

### Isolation and Infection Prevention
- Standard isolation period: 10 days from symptom onset plus 3 days after fever resolution (whichever is longer).
- Healthcare workers must use N95/FFP2 respirators, gowns, gloves, and eye protection during aerosol-generating procedures.
- Patient room should have negative pressure ventilation or natural ventilation with >= 12 air changes/hour.
- Viral shedding can persist for 7-12 days in mild cases and up to 20 days in severe/immunocompromised cases.
[Source: WHO IPC Guidelines for COVID-19, 2025 Update]

### Blood Work and Monitoring Schedule
- On admission: CBC, CRP, D-Dimer, Ferritin, LDH, Procalcitonin, Liver function tests, Renal function tests.
- D-Dimer > 1.0 mg/L is associated with higher mortality risk. Consider prophylactic anticoagulation (Enoxaparin 40mg SC daily).
- CRP trending: check every 48-72 hours. Rising CRP despite therapy suggests secondary bacterial infection or disease progression.
- Ferritin > 500 ng/mL indicates hyperinflammatory state — consider Tocilizumab or Baricitinib.
- Procalcitonin > 0.5 ng/mL suggests bacterial co-infection — initiate empirical antibiotics.
[Source: WHO COVID-19 Laboratory Testing Strategy, 2025]

### Post-COVID Follow-up Protocol
- Follow-up chest X-ray at 6 weeks post-discharge to assess radiological recovery.
- Pulmonary function tests (PFT) at 3 months if persistent dyspnea or abnormal follow-up imaging.
- Long COVID screening at 3, 6, and 12 months: assess for fatigue, cognitive impairment, dyspnea on exertion, chest pain.
- Cardiac evaluation (ECG + Echocardiography) if persistent tachycardia or chest pain at follow-up.
- Rehabilitation referral for patients with persistent functional impairment (6-minute walk test < 350m).
[Source: WHO Post COVID-19 Condition Clinical Case Definition, 2025]
