# Viral Pneumonia — Clinical Management Protocols

## Source: ATS/IDSA Community-Acquired Pneumonia Guidelines, 2024 Update

### Radiological Findings in Viral Pneumonia
- Bilateral interstitial infiltrates with patchy ground-glass opacities are the most common imaging pattern.
- Distribution predominantly in lower lung zones with peribronchial thickening.
- Diffuse bilateral involvement is typical of respiratory viruses (Influenza, RSV, Adenovirus, Parainfluenza).
- Consolidation is less common than in bacterial pneumonia but may develop in severe cases or with bacterial superinfection.
- Air bronchograms may be present within areas of consolidation.
- Pleural effusion is rare in pure viral pneumonia; its presence suggests bacterial co-infection or alternative diagnosis.
[Source: ATS/IDSA CAP Guidelines 2024, Radiological Assessment Section 4.1]

### Treatment Protocol — Antiviral Therapy
- Influenza pneumonia: Oseltamivir 75mg orally twice daily for 5 days. Most effective when started within 48 hours of symptom onset. Can be extended to 10 days in severe cases.
- RSV pneumonia (adults): Ribavirin may be considered in immunocompromised patients. Supportive care is primary for immunocompetent adults.
- Adenovirus pneumonia: Cidofovir 5mg/kg IV weekly for severe cases in immunocompromised patients.
- Parainfluenza: No specific antiviral therapy available. Supportive care with oxygen and bronchodilators.
- Empirical antibiotics (Amoxicillin-Clavulanate or Azithromycin) should be considered if bacterial co-infection cannot be ruled out.
[Source: IDSA Infectious Disease Guidelines, Viral Pneumonia Management 2024]

### Supportive Care Protocol
- Oxygen therapy: maintain SpO2 92-96%. Use nasal cannula (1-6 L/min) as first-line. Escalate to HFNC if inadequate.
- Bronchodilators: Salbutamol nebulization (2.5-5mg) every 4-6 hours for patients with wheezing or bronchospasm.
- Antipyretics: Paracetamol 500-1000mg every 6 hours for fever > 38.5C. Avoid NSAIDs as first-line in acute respiratory infection.
- IV fluid management: Isotonic saline at maintenance rate. Avoid fluid overload — monitor intake/output strictly.
- Cough management: Dextromethorphan 15-30mg every 6-8 hours for non-productive cough. Honey-based remedies may supplement.
[Source: British Thoracic Society Guidelines for Management of CAP, 2024]

### Severity Assessment — CURB-65 Score
- Confusion: new mental confusion (1 point)
- Urea: blood urea > 7 mmol/L (1 point)
- Respiratory rate: >= 30 breaths/min (1 point)
- Blood pressure: systolic < 90 mmHg or diastolic <= 60 mmHg (1 point)
- Age: >= 65 years (1 point)
- Score 0-1: outpatient treatment. Score 2: consider short hospitalization. Score 3-5: hospitalize, consider ICU.
[Source: BTS CAP Severity Assessment, CURB-65 Validation Studies]

### Follow-up Schedule
- Temperature and SpO2 monitoring twice daily during acute phase.
- Blood tests (CBC, CRP) at 1 week to assess treatment response.
- Follow-up chest X-ray at 2-3 weeks after diagnosis to check resolution.
- Pulmonology consultation if symptoms persist beyond 2 weeks or imaging shows worsening.
- Emergency criteria: SpO2 < 92%, severe chest pain, hemoptysis, altered consciousness, cyanosis (blue lips/fingertips).
- 6-week follow-up for complete recovery assessment and repeat imaging.
- Vaccination after recovery: annual influenza vaccine + pneumococcal vaccine (PCV20 or PPSV23) for at-risk patients.
[Source: ATS Post-Pneumonia Follow-up Recommendations, 2024]

### Prognosis and Recovery
- Typical recovery: 1-3 weeks for mild-moderate cases with supportive care.
- Most patients make a complete recovery with no residual lung damage.
- Young, previously healthy patients: excellent outcomes (mortality < 1%).
- Elderly or immunocompromised: higher risk of complications (mortality 5-15% depending on pathogen).
- Full lung function recovery expected within 4-8 weeks in uncomplicated cases.
- Post-illness fatigue lasting 2-4 weeks is common and expected — reassure patients this is normal.
[Source: Lancet Respiratory Medicine, Viral Pneumonia Outcomes Meta-Analysis 2024]
